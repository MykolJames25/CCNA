Communication across a network is carried on a medium. The medium provides the channel over which the message
travels from source to destination.
Modern networks primarily use three types of media to interconnect devices and to provide the pathway over which data
can be transmitted.
.
Metallic wires within cables - data is encoded into electrical impulses
.
Glass or plastic fibers (fiber optic cable) - data is encoded as pulses of light
.
Wireless transmission - data is encoded using wavelengths from the electromagnetic spectrum
